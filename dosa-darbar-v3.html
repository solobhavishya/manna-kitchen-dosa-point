A Kitchen That Time Chose Not to Forget
Dehradun · Since 1962

It Started With A Tawa

In a two-room house in Mylapore, Chennai, Kamala Iyer woke every morning at 4am to begin grinding. Not because she had to — because that was the only way the batter came out right. Granite on granite, slow and rhythmic, until the batter was exactly the right consistency — neither too thin nor too thick, fermenting in a clay vessel by evening, ready for morning service.

Her husband Rajan Iyer opened a small tiffin stall outside their home in 1962. Word spread the way it does in Chennai — quietly, through chai conversations and temple visits. Within a year, people were arriving before sunrise just to guarantee a plate.

When the family relocated to Dehradun, they brought the batter formula, the tawa, and the stubbornness never to take shortcuts. Three generations later, the recipe is still not written down. It never needed to be.

"
"Our batter is ground at 4am. Our recipes were never written down. That's not a flaw. That's the whole point."

Our Journey

1962

Founded in Chennai by Kamala & Rajan Iyer. A stone grinder, a tawa, and three chutneys was all it took.

1978

The recipe book burned in a house fire. Nothing was lost — every recipe existed perfectly in memory. Still does.

1991

First Dehradun outlet opened. The same batter formula made the journey to the foothills of the Himalayas.

2004

Second outlet opens, serving a growing city that has discovered its love for filter coffee and stone-ground batter.

2018

Third generation joins the kitchen. The batter is still ground the same way, at the same hour.

2024

MANNA KITCHEN DOSA POINT becomes Dehradun's most-reviewed South Indian restaurant. The recipe remains unwritten.

01
Stone-Ground Batter

Our batter is ground fresh every day using the same granite method Kamala Iyer used in 1962. It ferments overnight in clay vessels. You cannot replicate this with a blender. We've tried. Everyone has tried. This is the only way.

02
No Preservatives, Ever

Nothing in our kitchen lasts more than a day. The chutney you eat was ground this morning. The sambhar was slow-cooked before dawn. Freshness is not a marketing claim here — it is the only way we know how to work.

03
Filter Coffee Culture

True filter coffee requires a decoction slow-brewed for forty minutes, mixed with full-fat milk, and poured between a davara and tumbler until the froth is perfect. Ours is done exactly this way. All day. Every day.
